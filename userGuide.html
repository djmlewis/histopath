Using the Histology - Pathology Picture Quiz
The Histopathology Picture Quiz consist of a collection of unique cards - one for each pathological or histological specimen. The cards are grouped according to the image set and can be accessed using the dropdown menus on the top toolbar.
Click the arrows (or tap keyboard arrows) to show the previous / next card - the position will not move between the topics ("chapters").
Tap the hide button to hide the rightmost menu when testing to avoid remembering the numbering.
Click the book button to show the Cards Index.
Use the buttons in the header to choose to Family/Genus or Species, sorted either by name A-Z, or by taxonomy. Click an entry to show the card. Use the browser's inbuilt search (usually cmd-F) to find entries on the page. Several Species entries may direct to the same card. Click the close button or tap Esc on the keyboard to close the window.
Click the sidebar button to toggle the visibility of the sidebar with the Species Index. Click an entry to show the card. Several Species entries may direct to the same card. Use the browser's inbuilt search (usually cmd-F) to find entries on the sidebar.
Click to reveal a dropdown menu - select an option to open a new browser window/tab with pictures and captions. Use the browser's inbuilt search (usually cmd-F) to find pictures on the page. The pictures match the Picture Quiz chapters plus one with all images in the Parasites Atlas. The images are listed in alphabetical order of Family/Genus.
Use slider to distribute width between images and text panels. Slide to far right to show only images with the text panel hidden.
When the mouse hovers over an image the zoom cursor appears. Click on an image to zoom it full screen. Click on the left / right edges to move to previous / next image on this card. Click the close button or tap Esc on the keyboard to close the window.
Use the switches to have the images () or the captions () initially hidden when the card loads. Toggling a switch has the immediate effect on displayed card. Initially hiding the captions is important when testing - to reveal a caption click the grey caption bar. To hide / reveal all the images click .
Use the switch to have the text entries initially hidden when the card loads. Toggling the switch has the immediate effect on displayed card. Initially hiding the text is important when testing - to reveal/hide a single row click the label for that row.
To temporarily reveal or hide all the text click .
Testing Image Recognition
The rightmost menu now only displays "Image 1" etc. (Click the hide button to hide even that). The left dropdown menu offers groups of images to test. The text area remains the same and only one image per card is displayed if that card has more than one image associated with it. The purpose is to progress through the images in a chapter testing recognition. Use the switches to initially hide image captions and card text, or the slider to remove the text. Use the button to re-order the cards in that set and tape to move to the next random card, rather than the brown ← → buttons (see below)
Tap to reveal an uncluttered screen with just images and captions. Click the close button or tap Esc on the keyboard to close the window.
Use these buttons to categorise a card as:
Untested -- Incorrectly Identified -- Correctly Identified -- Favourite
The catogorisations are remembered by the browser. Use the dropdown to reset categorisations back to Untested.
These catogorisations can be used to include/exclude cards from testing (see Randomise below).
Set the and switches ON to initially hide image captions and card text.


Use the switches to include (switch on) or exclude (switch off) cards with the relevant category, then click Randomise to create a random list of images in this chapter -- the number remaining untested (including the displayed image) is shown on the right. If you change the switches tap Randomise again.

Identify the first image, tap the grey caption bar to reveal the answer, tap a category button to record.
Then tap to move to the next image on the list - the numbers remaining count down.
When the last image is viewed the system automatically re-randomises the list and starts again.
