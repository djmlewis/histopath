Using the Histology - Pathology Picture Quiz
The Histopathology Picture Quiz consist of a collection of unique cards - one for each pathological or histological specimen.
All the cards are in a single cardset but grouped into "chapters" such as "Histology" which can be accessed using the lefthand dropdown menu on the top toolbar.
Within each chapter cards are listed alphabetically by Diagnosis Organ/System Class such as "Dermatology", "Respiratory".
The righthand menu has the diagnosis obscured and lists the cards just as "Image 1, Image 2" (within this chapter) to assist with testing.
Only one image per card is displayed, and some cards may repeat within a chapter if it has more than one image.
Use the righthand menu or the brown arrow buttons to move between cards in a chapter.
Click the arrows (or tap keyboard arrows) to show the previous / next card - the position will not move between the "chapters".
Tap the hide button to hide the righthand menu when testing to avoid remembering the numbering.
Click the sidebar button to toggle the visibility of the sidebar with the Diagnoses Index. Click an entry to show the image and card. Diagnoses may be repeated if a card has multiple images.
Type in the box to find diagnoses in the sidebar that match the text exactly - diagnoses will disappear as you type more text as it must be an exact match, delete text to widen the search. If the sidebar is hidden it appears. Tap the diagnosis you want to open the card.
Tap clear button to reset sidebar to show all diagnoses.
Alternatively, use the browser's inbuilt search (usually cmd-F) to find entries on the sidebar.
Use slider to distribute width between images and text panels. Slide to far right to show only images with the text panel hidden.
When the mouse hovers over an image the zoom cursor appears. Click on an image to zoom it full screen.
Click the close button or tap Esc on the keyboard to close the window.
Use the Fit width and Fit height buttons to enlarge/shrink the image. Scrollbars will appear if the image is too wide or tall to fit the window.
Use the switches to have the captions initially hidden (switch on) or visble (switch off) each time a new card loads.
Click the grey caption bar to reveal a hidden caption, or hide a visible one.
Toggling the switch has the immediate effect on displayed card.
Initially hiding the captions is important when testing.
The app remembers your choice and loads it next time it runs on the same browser and computer.
Use the switch to have the text entries initially hidden (switch on) or visble (switch off) each time a new card loads.
Click the label in the lefthand column of a row to reveal hidden text or hide visible text in that row.
Toggling the switch has the immediate effect on displayed card.
Initially hiding the text is important when testing.
The app remembers your choice and loads it next time it runs on the same browser and computer.
Click a button to temporarily hide or reveal all the text on the displayed card.
Testing Image Recognition
The righthand menu only displays "Image 1" etc. (Click the hide button to hide even that numbering).
The left dropdown menu offers "chapters" of images to test. Only one image per card is displayed, and some cards may repeat within a chapter if it has more than one image.

The concept behind the app is to progress through the images in a chapter, testing recognition, which is described in detail in the sections below.
In overview:
• You can use the "Show / Hide" switches to initially hide image captions and card text.
• Optionally, use the Fullscreen button to focus the view, or set the slider to enlarge the images and remove the text.
• Use the button to re-order the cards in the selected chapter, including only cards selected by the category switches.
• Tap to move to the next random card rather than the brown ← → buttons.
Tap the Fullscreen button to reveal an uncluttered screen with just images and captions.
Tap the close button or tap Esc on the keyboard to close the window.
When testing yourself use the Category Buttons to categorise a card as:
Untested -- Incorrectly Identified -- Correctly Identified -- Favourite
These catogorisations can be used to include/exclude cards from testing (see Randomise below).
The catogorisations are remembered by the browser. Use the dropdown to reset all categorisations for a chapter or the whole cardset back to Untested.
Use the Category Switches to include (switch on) or exclude (switch off) cards with the relevant category. As you change the switches the app will re-Randomise the cards automatically using only categories that are switched on. The app remembers the switch settings and will reload those options next time it runs (on the same browser and same computer).
If the combination of Category Switches and Chapters (see below) results in all cards being excluded the app will display "No cards match", and the Randomise and Next Image buttons will be disabled.
Use the Chapter dropdown menu to include (selected - blue background) or exclude (deselected - white background) cards from the relevant chapters. Tap All or None buttons to add all or remove all chapters (tap None to clear the menu to speed-up just adding a few chapters).
As you change the selection the app will re-Randomise the cards automatically using only chapters that are selected.
If the combination of Category Switches (see above) and Chapters results in all cards being excluded the app will display "No cards match", and the Randomise and Next Image buttons will be disabled.
Running a Picture Quiz with the images in the selected chapter
Set the and switches ON to initially hide image captions and card text when it loads.



Tap Randomise to create a random list of images in this chapter (taking into account the Category Switches and Chapters dropdown menu settings).
The number of cards remaining untested is shown on the right.
Identify the image, tap the grey caption bar to reveal the answer, and (optionally) tap a Category Button (see above) to record correct/incorrect.
Tap to move to the next image on the shuffled list - the numbers remaining count down.
When the last image is viewed the system automatically re-randomises the list and starts again.
Repeatedly clicking Randomise may repeat images, whereas using to move to the next image in the shuffled list ensures you will be tested on all images only once.
