Searching for text in card titles or legend labels
Tap to open the search window to search for text in the legend labels or card titles.
Choose to display the card titles or legend labels. Card titles are grouped by cardset, legend labels are split into two columns -- English labels on right, Latin labels on left. Tap the a..z buttons to jump to labels starting with that letter. Tap on any label or title in the list and all the cards containing that title or label are listed below. Click on any card to load the card - a target will appear briefly to indicate the location of legend label.
View listed cards as small or large size using the switch in the window header. This setting applies to all windows.
Tap to close window, alternatively press Esc button on keyboard or tap in the background around the window.
Type exact text you want to find in labels and titles in the search box and click to start search. The number of matching legend labels and card titles is shown - this may match to fewer actual cards if the text is repeated on a card.
Search strings are “literal” - i.e. an exact match of the letters, punctuation  and spaces is made. The search text and all the labels are made lowercase to avoid problems where the search box may capitalise the first letter.  Partial strings will be found within whole words.  It is better to enter a partial stub instead of a whole word or phrase - long enough not to return too many hits. e.g. “semimembr” instead of “m. semimembranosus” or “semi”. Consider Latin and English differences e.g “carotis” or “carotidis” versus “carotid artery” will be found using “caroti” as the search. Use spaces to identify words - e.g. “ axillaris” finds “a. axillaris” and “v. axillaris” but not “maxillaris” (nor “axillary vein”).
When you have made a search tap on one of the rows (text is blue to indicate a filtered selection) or tap to list all the cards matching the search. Use the switches to select whether to show cards with matching text in legend labels and/or card title. Click on any card to load.
Tap to clear the search box, clear all listed cards, reset search.
The listed cards found by a search can be saved directly to an existing or new Bookmarks List. Either enter a valid new name into the box (only letters, numbers, _ are allowed) or select an existing Bookmarks List from the menu, and tap the button.

Creating and Editing Post-it Notes
Post-it notes are rectangular labels with a fixed width that you can create and pin to a figure. They stay pinned by their top left corner to the same location even when you resize the figure or flip it (see below about how post-its respond to flipping the image). You can choose coloured, white or transparent notes with different font colours and sizes. Post-its are managed by the browser so it is important to Export them regularly to backup against data loss if the browser purges its cache - see above.
Tap to bring the Post-its design palette to the front.
Select a background colour, a font colour and size (the post-it width adjusts for the font size), and enter text in the box. To pin the post-it to the figure either tap the figure where you want the post-its top left corner to be pinned, or tap and then drag the post-it to the desired location. Tap to reset the palette to default values.
When you tap or drag a post-it the palette is filled with its content. If you change the colours or text tap Update to update the post-it, or Cancel to clear the palette. The post-it always stays where you drag it.
Post-its will flip when you flip a figure, pinned by their top left corner. This means they obscure the same area of the figure when flipped - although the post-it does not change size when the figure does. If you prefer to always keep the text horizonal deselect this option, which means the post-it will cover a different part of a flipped figure and may be partially cut-off if pinned near the edges. To minimise this some restrictions are imposed when dragging to a new location.. This setting persists between sessions.
  
Post-its are pinned to the figure by their top left corner - this anchor corner is marked by when editing to assist with accurate location of the "pin" that anchors the post-it to the figure. Note that when the post-it is flipped the anchor corner will appear on the bottom left of the flipped post-it but it is still the top left corner of the post-it as the text reads.
Creating and Editing Annotations
Unlike post-its which are fixed size, annotations are "drawn" onto the figure itself and so shrink, grow and rotate with the figure. Annotations are managed by the browser so it is important to Export them regularly to backup against data loss if the browser purges its cache - see above.
Tap to bring the Annotations design palette to the front. Tap to reset the palette to default values and clear the text area.
To write a text block click on the A in the selector. Click on the black/grey/white buttons or drag the slider to choose a colour for the text and background. Drag the transparency sliders to make the text and background transparent, and the size slider to change font size. Type some text in the text area - use returns to break into lines as it will not wrap - then click on the figure where you want the text.
To draw an ellipse, rectangle or line click on the shape in the selector - the colours palette will adjust to the type selected. Click on the black/grey/white buttons or drag the slider to choose a colour for the shape fill and border/line. Drag the transparency sliders to make the fill/border/line more or less transparent, and the thickness slider to change border or line thickness. For lines use the dropdown menu to select an end shape. Click on the figure where you want the shape.
When you add or click on an annotation "handles" appear to show it has been selected, and the palette is setup with its colours, sizes and text. Use the handles to delete, resize and rotate the shape. When rotated the resize action may apear reversed. With text resize increases font size.
When you have finished adjusting the palette values click Update to copy them to the shape, or Done to deselect the shape with no changes. Clicking anywhere on the figure also deselects the shape.
Click on an annotation and drag it to where you want it. With lines you must click on the line - not any end shape.
Tap to send the currently selected annotation to the back - so you can select and edit annotations it obscures.

Topics Lists
Topics are Bookmarks Lists which have been pre-prepared and can be viewed directly from the Topics List using shift-arrow buttons, or imported as a new Bookmarks List for you to edit. Topics are based on UVMP chapters. The purpose is to draw attention to similar figures in other cardsets which might otherwise be missed, by placing them alongside complementary UVMP figures. This allows the full potential of the app to be used. Not all UVMP figures will be included in a Topics List - only those with complementary figures in other cardsets. Topics are in development and new titles will be added and updated periodically -- note the version number of the lists on the menu and ones you have imported. Once imported, the resulting Bookmarks List can be edited, merged, renamed etc. You can create your own form of Topic Lists using the Bookmarks Gallery to add bookmarks to a Bookmark List and then export the list as a file available to import again if you clear the browser cache, switch computer or browser, or shared with other users. The browser may also automatically clear its cache (unless is shown at the bottom of the screen in which case Bookmarks will only be deleted if you manually clear the browser cache).
Tap to show the Topics List.  Select a Topics List title from the drop-down menu and click on a card to load it.
View listed cards as small or large size using the switch in the window header. This setting applies to all windows.
Tap to close window, or press Esc button or tap in the background around the window.
Tap to import the Topics List as a new Bookmarks List with the Topics List name (plus extra suffix if a Bookmarks List of the same name already exists), which can be edited as any Bookmarks List. Create your own version of Topics Lists using the Bookmarks Gallery to add bookmarks to a new Bookmarks List and then export the list as a file available to share with other users or import again (exported Bookmark Lists appear in the Bookmarks Gallery when imported and not the Topics lists menu).

If you navigate to a card not in the currently selected Topics List these buttons are disabled (see Navigating through Bookmarks and Topics Lists).
Navigating through Bookmarks and Topics Lists
The diagram illustrates the path taken when you use the toolbar arrow buttons or the ↑ ↓ Shift-↑ Shift-↓ keys on the keyboard to navigate through the currently selected Bookmarks or Topics list (white arrows on the diagram). The 5 bookmarked cards in this list come from two cardsets and four different chapters, so the selection jumps around as you navigate using the toolbar arrow buttons or ↑ ↓ keys to move to the next card in the list. The cards in the Bookmarks / Topics list follow the order in the Bookmarks / Topics Gallery, so the ↑ key takes you to the previous bookmark and the ↓ key to the next.
If you use toolbar buttons or ←  → keys (brown arrows on diagram) and this takes you to a card that is in the same Bookmarks or Topics list (e.g. card A 1-002→ and card A 1-003← in the diagram) you can continue using the ↑ ↓ keys to traverse the list. Testing Legend Label Identification
Hotspots -- quick test
If a card has hotspots hover over the label once you have identified it to reveal the answer. To avoid seeing answers as the mouse moves use Settings to have labels appear only when the hotspot is clicked. To test cards within a chapter in random sequence click the random button.
Self Test Label Buttons -- record answers
Use Settings to show self test buttons

which appear across the top of the screen:

Choose a label and decide which anatomical feature it labels. Tap the corresponding button and a window appears revealing the label text. Tap a button to record your answer as correct or incorrect, or no score. Your answers are saved in the browser cache and displayed whenever the card loads allowing you to focus on anatomical features you have recalled incorrectly.
Importing, Exporting and Clearing Saved Answers
If you switch browser or clear the cache your recorded answers are lost, but can be exported to the computer as a file and imported again using the Share Menu. The browser may also automatically clear its cache (unless is shown at the bottom of the screen in which case Saved Answers will only be deleted if you manually clear the browser cache). Microsoft Edge may refuse to export due to security settings - use Chrome. Use the Share Menu to clear recorded answers.
Figure Legend -- clicking
Use Settings to select these options:
Show the figure legend by tapping the button . When the card appears, instead of the whole legend appearing, only the first label is shown with the anatomical feature text replaced by “?→”. A target will appear on the figure to locate the label. Decide which feature it labels and then click the green button at the top of the legend . The full label is revealed, followed by the next label and “?→”. This process can be repeated for all the other labels in numerical order each time you tap the button. Repeat until all labels are identified. No scores are recorded - this is a methodical test. To reset the test to the beginning click the grey ⓧ button at any time. As it is possible to learn a sequence of labels rather than the actual label-anatomical feature relationship, use Settings to have the labels appear in a random order.

Preloading images
When selected this option will result in all images in the same chapter, Bookmarks List or Search result that are not already in the browser cache being downloaded when you select one.
